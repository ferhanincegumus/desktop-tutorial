Provenance

Why the Black Sea.

A region of forests, mountains, and centuries-old beekeeping tradition — largely untouched by industrial agriculture.

Pine Forests, Coastal Belt

Along the misted coastline, dense pine forests create the rare conditions needed for honeydew honey — where bees gather secretions left by forest insects rather than nectar alone.

The result is a slow, deliberate harvest, guided by generations of traditional beekeeping knowledge passed through families of the region.

Chestnut Highlands, Mountain Interior

Further inland, wild chestnut trees blanket the mountain slopes in early summer. Their brief flowering window demands precise timing — a tradition local beekeepers have refined for generations.

The altitude and mineral-rich soil give this honey its distinctive depth and structure.

Wildflower Meadows, Open Country

Between forest and coast, undisturbed meadows bloom with dozens of native wildflower species. No two harvests are quite the same — each shaped by that season's particular bloom.

This diversity of forage is a signal of a healthy, largely untouched landscape.
